# Holtek HT24LC08 / HT24LC02 2-wire serial EEPROM

## 1.1 Overview

The Holtek HT24LC08 is an **8-Kbit (1024 × 8) CMOS serial EEPROM** with the
industry-standard 24Cxx two-wire (I2C) interface: 1.8–5.5 V operation,
automatic erase-before-write, a 16-byte page-write mode, a hardware
write-protect pin, 10⁶ rewrite cycles per word and 40-year data retention
[HT24LC08 DS p.1](#sources). Its smaller sibling, the **HT24LC02**, is the
2-Kbit (256 × 8) member of the same family with an 8-byte page
[HT24LC02 DS p.1](#sources).

The ASUS KGPE-D16 populates one of each, identified by the schematic
part-description field — `U25` is
"EEPROM HT24LC08-SOP-NPB-TR SO8//HOLTEK 8K 2.7V"
[QU1_pins.md:255](https://github.com/mithro/ai-shenanigans-for-bmcs/blob/main/asus-kgpe-d16-firmware/schematic-wiring/pinmaps/QU1_pins.md#L255):

- **`U25` (HT24LC08)** — the **board FRU EEPROM** (serial/part data), alone
  with the two DIMM-LED expanders on the AST2050 BMC's I2C5
  [AST2050-BMC-WIRING.md:352](https://github.com/mithro/ai-shenanigans-for-bmcs/blob/main/asus-kgpe-d16-firmware/schematic-wiring/AST2050-BMC-WIRING.md#L352).
- **`ZU2` (HT24LC02)** — the **private configuration EEPROM** of the LSI FW322
  FireWire controller, on a two-wire bus that never touches the management
  fabric
  [I2C-SMBUS-TOPOLOGY.md:190-200](https://github.com/mithro/ai-shenanigans-for-bmcs/blob/main/asus-kgpe-d16-firmware/schematic-wiring/I2C-SMBUS-TOPOLOGY.md#L190-L200).

**`U25` wiring.** The EEPROM's pin 5 (`SDA`) and pin 6 (`SCL`) — exactly the
datasheet's 8-SOP assignment [HT24LC08 DS p.1](#sources) — wire to AST2050
balls A13/B13 (nets `N39526313`/`N39526322`), shared only with the two W83601G
DIMM-error-LED expanders `U27`/`U28` ({doc}`w83601g`)
[QU1_pins.md:262-266](https://github.com/mithro/ai-shenanigans-for-bmcs/blob/main/asus-kgpe-d16-firmware/schematic-wiring/pinmaps/QU1_pins.md#L262-L266),
[I2C-SMBUS-TOPOLOGY.md:154-156](https://github.com/mithro/ai-shenanigans-for-bmcs/blob/main/asus-kgpe-d16-firmware/schematic-wiring/I2C-SMBUS-TOPOLOGY.md#L154-L156).
The topology is deliberate: DIMM SPD EEPROMs also answer in the `0x50`–`0x57`
range, so the board isolates `U25` on its own I2C5 segment while the SPD banks
live behind the `QU5` mux on separate segments — "the board EEPROM `U25` (also
`0x50`-range) is isolated on the separate `I2C5` segment for the same reason"
[AST2050-BMC-WIRING.md:397-401](https://github.com/mithro/ai-shenanigans-for-bmcs/blob/main/asus-kgpe-d16-firmware/schematic-wiring/AST2050-BMC-WIRING.md#L397-L401).
The netlist extract records only the two bus nets for `U25` ("8 pins, 2 nets"
[QU1_pins.md:255](https://github.com/mithro/ai-shenanigans-for-bmcs/blob/main/asus-kgpe-d16-firmware/schematic-wiring/pinmaps/QU1_pins.md#L255)),
so the WP strap is not captured there.

**`ZU2` wiring.** The LSI FW322 1394a controller has a private two-wire link to
`ZU2`, "from which it loads its 1394 GUID and configuration at power-up. This
bus is independent of the management fabric"
[I2C-SMBUS-TOPOLOGY.md:190-200](https://github.com/mithro/ai-shenanigans-for-bmcs/blob/main/asus-kgpe-d16-firmware/schematic-wiring/I2C-SMBUS-TOPOLOGY.md#L190-L200)
— neither the BMC nor the SP5100 can reach it.

**The FRU angle.** `U25` is where the board keeps its FRU-style identity data.
The standard layout for such an EEPROM — common header, then board/product
info areas carrying manufacturer, part number and serial number — is the
[IPMI Platform Management FRU Information Storage Definition](https://www.intel.com/content/dam/www/public/us/en/documents/product-briefs/platform-management-fru-document-rev-1-2-feb-2013.pdf)
([archived](https://web.archive.org/web/20221023104159/https://www.intel.com/content/dam/www/public/us/en/documents/product-briefs/platform-management-fru-document-rev-1-2-feb-2013.pdf)).
At 1024 bytes the 8-Kbit part comfortably holds a FRU image; an open BMC
firmware would expose it through the at24 driver (§1.6) and, on the OpenBMC
stack, [`entity-manager`](https://github.com/openbmc/entity-manager)'s FRU
probing.

## 1.4 Device addressing: the block-select bits

Every transaction opens with the 24Cxx device-address byte: a mandatory
`1010` prefix, three device-select bits, and the R/W̄ bit
[HT24LC08 DS p.4](#sources):

`1 0 1 0 | A2 | A1 | A0 | R/W̄`

The two parts fill the three middle bits differently:

- **HT24LC02** — all three bits are compared against the hard-wired `A0`–`A2`
  pins, so up to eight devices share one bus; an unconnected pin reads as low
  [HT24LC02 DS p.4](#sources).
- **HT24LC08** — only the `A2` bit is compared against the `A2` pin (the `A0`
  and `A1` pins have no internal connection); the two lower bits are **memory
  block ("page") addressing** — the most-significant bits b9:b8 of the 10-bit
  word address. At most two 8-Kbit devices share a bus
  [HT24LC08 DS p.1,4](#sources).

The 8-Kbit part therefore *occupies four consecutive 7-bit addresses*, one per
256-byte block. `U25` answers at `0x50`–`0x53`
[AST2050-BMC-WIRING.md:352](https://github.com/mithro/ai-shenanigans-for-bmcs/blob/main/asus-kgpe-d16-firmware/schematic-wiring/AST2050-BMC-WIRING.md#L352),
i.e. its `A2` pin is strapped low:

```{list-table} HT24LC08 block select (A2 = 0, as strapped on U25)
:header-rows: 1
:widths: 40 25 35

* - Device-address byte
  - 7-bit address
  - Block addressed
* - `1010 0 0 0 x`
  - `0x50`
  - bytes `0x000`–`0x0FF`
```

## 1.5 Operations

Both parts implement the standard 24Cxx operation set
[HT24LC08 DS p.5-6](#sources), [HT24LC02 DS p.5-6](#sources):

- **Byte write** — device-address byte, one word-address byte, one data byte,
  STOP; the STOP starts the internally-timed write cycle (≤ 5 ms), during
  which all inputs are disabled and the device does not respond.
- **Page write** — as byte write but up to 15 (HT24LC08) / 7 (HT24LC02) more
  data bytes before the STOP. Only the low four (08) / three (02) word-address
  bits increment, so a write that runs past a page boundary **wraps within the
  same page** rather than advancing.
- **Acknowledge polling** — after starting a write cycle, the master may
  repeatedly send START + a write-command device-address byte; the device
  withholds ACK until the cycle completes, letting the master resume at the
  earliest moment instead of always waiting the full t<sub>WR</sub>.
- **Current-address read** — the internal address counter holds
  last-address + 1; a read-mode device-address byte clocks out that byte.
- **Random read** — a "dummy write" (device-address byte + word address, no
  data) sets the counter, then a repeated START and current-address read fetch
  the byte.
- **Sequential read** — while the master keeps ACKing, the device increments
  the address and streams data, rolling over at the end of the array; the
  master ends with NoACK + STOP.

**Write protect.** Tying WP to V<sub>CC</sub> write-protects the **full array**
(the device behaves as a serial ROM); WP at V<sub>SS</sub> or floating allows
normal read/write [HT24LC08 DS p.4-5](#sources),
[HT24LC02 DS p.4](#sources).

## 1.6 Linux at24 driver binding

```{admonition} Mainline EEPROM driver
:class: note

- **Driver**: [`drivers/misc/eeprom/at24.c`](https://github.com/torvalds/linux/blob/master/drivers/misc/eeprom/at24.c)
  (`CONFIG_EEPROM_AT24`) handles the whole 24Cxx class; the matching chip
  types are `24c08` (8192/8 bytes) for `U25` and `24c02` for the (BMC-unreachable) `ZU2`.
- **Device tree**: compatible `"atmel,24c08"` per the
  [`at24.yaml`](https://github.com/torvalds/linux/blob/master/Documentation/devicetree/bindings/eeprom/at24.yaml)
  binding — 24Cxx compatibles use the generic Atmel names regardless of the
  actual vendor, so a Holtek part binds as `atmel,24c08`.
- The driver understands the multi-address block-select scheme (§1.4): it
  reserves the extra I2C addresses with dummy clients ("some chips tie up
  multiple I2C addresses; dummy devices reserve them") and presents one flat
  1024-byte NVMEM/sysfs `eeprom` device.
- A KGPE-D16 BMC device tree therefore places an `eeprom@50` node with
  `compatible = "atmel,24c08"` under the AST2050's I2C5 controller.
```
